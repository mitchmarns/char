one of the perks of being an only child is having all of the attention on you all the time. picture-perfect weekends spent fishing on the lake or at the rink practicing, learning to love the same game that his father had. one of the drawbacks of being an only child was… well, all the attention was on you. from an early age, taylor idolized his father, doing anything he could to impress him, even if it meant pushing his relationship with his mother to the side. to him, his father was his best friend, his idol – his everything.

But that bond was more fragile than taylor could have imagined. His father didn’t look at him like a son, but a project – someone to mold and push, to carry on the legacy of excellence that his father had started. Taylor had always been intelligent, but he lacked confidence in his academic abilities. No matter how hard he tried, he could never meet the high expectations that his father had set. Hockey was the only thing that connected them when everything else was stripped away, but even that came with conditions.

When taylor was nine, he had the chance to play on a team far better than the one where he lived in Cambridge. The only problem was that the team was in Michigan; easily solved when a friend from tournaments offered him a place to stay. Their parents all agreed with each other that the arrangement would work fine as a temporary solution. However, this situation became permanent when his parents simply decided they didn’t want to take him back. His father, tired of Taylor’s rebellious streak, his impulsive decisions, his emotional outbursts, his struggles with self-harm that had presented at a young age, pushed for it and his mother went along with it. Just like that… he was no longer their responsibility.

Living with chase was the first time that he was really exposed to what family really felt like, but still, even though their home was always filled with warmth, he always felt just a little bit alone. Chase’s mother, a single mother who worked long hours as a nurse to provide everything for her two sons and the young boy she had been stuck with. No one – not chase, his mother, or his brother – ever made taylor feel that he was an outsider though. Chase, most of all, was always by his side – through every bad decision, reckless night, and downward spiral. Their friendship wasn’t just important to taylor – it was the foundation of his life.

Taylor made a lot of questionable choices over the years – partying too hard, falling into toxic relationships, struggling with substance abuse – but he sees it all as part of his journey. If nothing else, its proved to himself that he’s become his own person. Not the one that his father shaped him into – and maybe that was always the point.
